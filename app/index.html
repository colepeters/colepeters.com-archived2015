Hello

I am a designer, writer, and sound artist. I believe that design and the written word are tools — and that what we do with these tools is as important as the tools themselves.

My current and ongoing obsessions include ethical design, investigative journalism, literature, simplicity, digital communications, things made by hand, and challenging the status quo.

Recent work

First Things First 2014
firstthingsfirst2014.org

In 1964, Ken Garland published what I consider to be one of the most important historical documents in design: the First Things First manifesto. 50 years later, I took the initiative to renew it — and to open it up to designers, developers, and multi-disciplinary communicators everywhere to sign.

The 2014 edition of the manifesto brings questions of ethics in design and technology up to date with contemporary concerns like online privacy and ownership of data; it addresses the lack of diversity in the industry; and it challenges us to more closely examine the work we choose to put into the world.

Within its first month of being online, the manifesto counted over 800 signatures in its list — a list that continues to grow. It has received coverage from Eye magazine, Design Week, Adbusters, and Fast Co.Exist. I gave a talk on the project at the March edition of Design and Banter; you can read a write-up on the talk right here.

This project is not meant to remain static; stay tuned for further developments and initiatives.

A Desiccated History of Ideas
colepeters.bandcamp.com

Recorded in August 2013 in London, England, and released digitally the November following, A Desiccated History of Ideas is a study in extreme high- and low-spectrum frequencies and amorphous sound structures. Composed entirely of tape loops, field recordings, and a carefully controlled mixing board feeding back on itself, the album is at turns ghostly silent and deafening.

The three extended compositions use sparse, searing structures of various intensity and dynamics to explore the concepts of banality and purpose, inhabitant and environment — and where the lines between these things begin to disintegrate.

In order to fully appreciate this recording, it should be played at loud volumes; however, due to the extreme range in frequencies, please be warned that there is a risk of damage to your hearing (and that of your pets) when reproduced without caution.

Rugby
rugby.colepeters.com

Rugby is a tiny web app that converts RGB colour swatches from 8-bit integers to percentage-based values, which makes life easier for designers who need to share their work with Objective-C developers.

I originally built Rugby as an excuse to learn Ruby on Rails, but several months later decided to rewrite it in much-simpler Javascript. I even managed to work in some tasteful UI touches (like the RGB input text changing to match the colour that’s being entered) thanks to some JS magic.
