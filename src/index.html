Hello

Up until the end of 2014, I would’ve opened this web page by telling you that I am a designer and a writer, primarily interested in the intersectioon of culture and technology. But as 2015 kicks into gear, I’m not so sure that these labels match up with me any longer.

After an extended hiatus, I have recently found myself gravitating back to the world of photography, and away from the hyper-modern miasma of technology. I don’t know what this means for the future of myself or my work. But I’m excited to find out.

Consider this web page in a state of flux. As with the rest of this story.

You’ll find a selection of my work below.

Photography

Photography

In A New Light

colepeters.exposure.co/in-a-new-light
This is the story of my falling in love with photography, how I left it behind, and how I found my way back to it. Delivered in 1,740 words, with a collection of photographs spanning the years of 2007–2015.

VSCO Grid & Journal

cole.vsco.co
I’ve been a devoted user of VSCO’s iOS camera app, VSCO Cam, for a long time. I recently started processing photographs taken with my Nikon using their film emulation software, VSCO Film, and sharing selected results on my Grid.

In addition to this, I’ve begun creating entries in my Journal, combining writing with images from my Grid.

Writing

Writing

Blog

blog.colepeters.com
I have been writing on the subjects of design, technology, and culture, and questioning how they influence, promote, or suppress one another, since 2012. Interspersed among these articles are an assortment of more personal, inward-looking documents. As my creative focus finds a new footing, so too, I imagine, will the focus of my blog. Stay tuned.

Published writing

I’ve been very fortunate to have some of my writing published by people other than myself. My writing and commentary have appeared in:

  • Offscreen Magazine, Issue 9
    I wrote “The Weight of Ingenuity”, a short article on the technology industry’s preference for “new” over “better”, by invitation from Offscreen’s publisher, Kai Brach. I was honoured to have my article open this issue of his wonderful publication.
  • VoxPop: “Do you think design can save the world?”
    I was asked to write a brief response to this question alongside several others in this edition of Design Week’s feature commentary section.
